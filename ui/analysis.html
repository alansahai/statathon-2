Descriptive Statistics

What it does: Provides basic summary statistics including mean, median, standard deviation, min/max values, and distribution characteristics for numeric columns.

Best for: Understanding the overall shape and spread of your data, identifying central tendencies, and spotting potential data quality issues.
🔀

Crosstab Analysis

What it does: Compares two categorical variables to understand relationships and patterns. Creates a contingency table showing how categories intersect.

Best for: Analyzing relationships between groups (e.g., gender vs. income bracket, region vs. product preference).
📉

Regression Analysis

What it does: Examines relationships between variables and makes predictions. Identifies how changes in one variable affect another.

Best for: Understanding cause-and-effect relationships, making predictions, and identifying key factors that influence outcomes.
